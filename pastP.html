About Us

Pioneering legal excellence in the heart of Anambra State

Who We Are

The Awka Branch of the Nigerian Bar Association, popularly called "The Capital Bar", is one of the many branches of the Nigerian Bar Association. It is the second oldest branch of the eight (8) branches of the NBA in Anambra State of Nigeria.

Our Location

Located in the capital of Anambra State—the Awka Capital Territory—hence the appellation "The Capital Bar". We serve as the premier legal institution in the state capital.

Our History

A journey of resilience, growth, and legal excellence since 1971

01

Formation & Registration

1971 - The Beginning

The Awka Branch of the Nigerian Bar Association was formed after the Nigerian Civil War, precisely in the year 1971. Prior to this, the territory that now constitutes the Awka Judicial Division of the High Court of Anambra State was part of the Onitsha Judicial Division of the High Court of the then Eastern Region.

There were no law offices open in Awka prior to the Nigerian civil war. However, as a result of the war, Nigerian lawyers of Igbo extraction practising in various parts of Nigeria were forced to return to their home base. Consequently, lawyers including George Nwogbonnia Amankemekwu Okafor (popularly called Amanke), the first General Secretary of the Nigerian Bar Association, relocated from Lagos to Awka. He was joined by James Nnajiofor Iloduba Ezekwe, the first lawyer called to the Bar from Nibo town, who relocated from Jos to Awka.

The Founding Fathers

Ozo G.N.A. Okafor Esq.

"Amanke"

Founding Chairman (1971-1987). First General Secretary of the NBA. First indigene of Awka town called to the Nigerian Bar.

J.N.I. Ezekwe Esq.

Founding Vice Chairman

First lawyer called to the Bar from Nibo town. Later served as Chairman (1987-1990).

P.N. Nwofor Esq.

"Lord Ogmor"

Founding Secretary from Nawfia. Instrumental in establishing branch operations.

Chief (Sir) O.B. Onyali SAN

Founding Assistant Secretary

Later Secretary and Chairman (1990-1999). Now the Leader of the Branch and only surviving founding member.

Chief (Sir) Benedict Ozoemena Onyali

SAN

Prominent among the 1966 law students enrolled into Nigerian Law School, called to Bar in 1971.

Other Founding Members

Including Barr. Ikejiani (Nri), G.C. Nonyelu (Amawbia), F.K.C. Nonyelu (Amawbia), Chief P.N. Obegolu, and Charles Udemezue Ikejiofor.

Growth & Expansion

Since formation in 1971 and registration with the National body of the NBA, Awka Branch has grown from strength to strength and has produced other branches of the NBA.

  • Aguata Branch of the NBA (The Frontline Bar)
  • Anaocha Branch of the NBA

Notable Achievements

  • ▸ Hosted National Executive Committee Meetings of the NBA
  • ▸ Hosted Eastern Bar Forum (EBF) Meetings
  • ▸ Established permanent Bar Centre at 50 Court Road, Awka
  • ▸ Annual celebration of Chief (Sir) O.B. Onyali SAN, our surviving founder

Secretariat & Meeting Venues

Evolution of Our Home

Initial Days (1971-1980s)

Meetings held at Chief Magistrate Court Awka

Transition Period (1980s-2017)

Moved to State Judiciary Headquarters Conference Hall

Current Home (2017-Present)

During Chief Emmanuel Odum's regime (2015-2017), NBA Awka Branch moved into the present Awka Bar compound. We currently operate from a temporary structure while building our permanent structure at the Ozo Amanke Okafor Bar Centre.
